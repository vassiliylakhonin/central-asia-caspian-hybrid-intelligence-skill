# Review checklist

A lightweight review checklist for outputs produced with this skill. This is a **review checklist**, not a validated benchmark. It does not score correctness; it helps a reviewer notice missing structure, evidence discipline, or scope problems.

Run through these questions for any non-trivial Central Asia / Caspian memo:

## Scope and framing

- Is the geography scoped correctly? Is Central Asia the core frame?
- If the Caspian is included, is its inclusion justified by a specific transmission channel?
- If Russia, China, EU, UK, US, the Gulf or the South Caucasus appear, do they change the mechanism, leverage or decision?
- Is the question framed as a concrete risk or strategy problem rather than a generic regional essay?

## Reasoning quality

- Does the answer explain **mechanism before implication**?
- Are transmission channels (banking, payments, routing, ownership, corridors) identified?
- Are actor incentives and leverage shifts addressed?
- Does the answer identify trigger points and watch-next indicators?
- Are role-based implications included for the relevant audience (bank, fintech, investor, operator, analyst)?

## Evidence discipline

- Is **evidence mode** stated explicitly (e.g. *evidence-backed*, *illustrative / reasoning-only*)?
- Are claims separated into `Verified` / `Plausible` / `Judgment` / `Unknown` where useful?
- Does the answer avoid fake legal/compliance certainty?
- Are sources of any factual claim attributable to a real source class (per `docs/source-guide.md`) — or labeled as illustrative?
- Are unknowns explicitly stated, including what would be required to resolve them?

## Tone and safety

- Does the answer avoid alarmism without a transmission channel?
- Does it avoid unsupported numerical precision?
- Does it use cautious language (`may consider`, `subject to review`, `depends on enforcement or regulation`) rather than determinative claims?
- For sanctions / AML / banking / legal topics, is a limitation note included?
- Does it avoid claims that the skill performs sanctions screening or live verification?

## Decision usefulness

- Does the answer state what would change the judgment?
- Does it provide a confidence footer (confidence level, key assumptions, main unknowns, indicators to watch)?
- Could a working analyst use it as a starting point for action without being misled?

## Hard fails

If any of these are true, the memo should be revised:

- Fabricated citations, list IDs, statute citations, or enforcement records.
- Determinative claims like *fully compliant*, *no risk*, *guaranteed*.
- Legal, sanctions, AML, tax or investment advice presented as such.
- Claims that the skill performs sanctions screening or live retrieval.
- Geography expanded for decoration without a mechanism.
